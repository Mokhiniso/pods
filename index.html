The question nobody asks out loud
"What if others mess it up?"

It's the biggest hidden objection. Here's how pods are structured so no single person can damage the group.

🗳️
Voting, not deciding
Every allocation requires a majority vote. No one person — including you — can move money unilaterally. The worst outcome is a tie, not a mistake.
No single-person control, ever
○
Predefined boundaries
Pods only invest in pre-approved categories — diversified index funds and ETFs. The worst possible decision is still a globally diversified portfolio.
No leverage, no single stocks
🧑‍🏫
Mentor as circuit breaker
Mentors earn only when the pod beats a benchmark. They're incentivised to flag poor decisions — and have no power to override yours without a vote.
Aligned incentives, not control
